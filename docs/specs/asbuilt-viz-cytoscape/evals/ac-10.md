# Eval AC10 — clusters pack tight; no empty middle

Observable success: Looking at the dense bundle fully zoomed out, the clusters
sit close to one another — the map reads as one coherent packed picture, not
islands scattered around a void. Measured mechanically: the settled layout's
overall bounding box is no more than 3× the summed area of the clusters
themselves (constant recorded with the test, tightened if the spike shows the
engine does better). The layout that got round one reverted — big groups
pushed far apart around a huge empty middle — measurably fails this bound.

Anti-patterns this catches: "Population-scaled anchor rings or any layout that
buys density relief with empty space between clusters" (spec anti-pattern #2,
the second field-reverted patch).

Would fail if:
- The two largest clusters sit far apart with a conspicuous void between them
  while the bounding box balloons past the packing bound.
- Compactness holds for small bundles but collapses on the dense fixture.
- The bound is "met" by shrinking clusters into unreadable specks instead of
  packing them — cluster contents must remain readable per AC2's zoom-reveal
  guarantee, which this eval inherits as a joint condition.
