# Eval AC10 — the approved compact look does not silently degrade

Observable success: Looking at the dense bundle fully zoomed out, the layout
matches the look approved by a human at the spike checkpoint (2026-07-16):
tight, discrete, named clusters — not islands scattered around a void.
Measured mechanically as a **drift anchor**: the settled layout's packing
factor (overall bounding box ÷ summed cluster areas) stays at or below 4.5,
with the checkpoint-measured value (4.116) recorded beside the bound in the
test. A regression that spreads clusters further apart than the approved
layout pushes the factor past the anchor and fails.

What this eval does NOT claim (amended 2026-07-16, checkpoint ruling): the
ratio is not a compactness discriminator — the spike measured the reverted
ring layout at 2.63 (it would "pass"), so catching ring-style empty-middle
layouts is the human look-gate's job, exercised at the spike checkpoint and
at final review, not this metric's.

Anti-patterns this catches: layout drift away from the approved rendering of
spec anti-pattern #2's fix — the engine or options changing such that
clusters drift measurably further apart than what the human approved.

Would fail if:
- A change to layout options or engine pushes the dense fixture's packing
  factor above 4.5 while tests still pass elsewhere.
- The bound is "met" by shrinking clusters into unreadable specks instead of
  packing them — cluster contents must remain readable per AC2's zoom-reveal
  guarantee, which this eval inherits as a joint condition.
- The recorded measured value is silently dropped from the test, leaving the
  anchor unauditable against future re-measurements.
